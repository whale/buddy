# Buddy Trademark Policy

Buddy's **code** is MIT-licensed — use it, fork it, sell things built with it. This
policy is about the **name and the sticker icon**, which are how people know they're
getting the real thing. (Modelled on [Ghost's trademark policy](https://ghost.org/trademark/),
whose software/name split this project also follows.)

## You can, without asking

- Fork the code, modify it, self-host it, and ship products built on it (MIT).
- Say your project is *"derived from the source code for Buddy"* or *"compatible
  with Buddy"*.
- Offer services **for** Buddy with the name after your own: *"Hosting for Buddy"*,
  *"Acme's sync service for Buddy"* — as long as it's clear the service doesn't
  come from Buddy's maintainer.
- Use the name in articles, reviews, tutorials, and talks.

## You can't, without written permission

- Call a fork, app, or service **"Buddy"**, or anything that reads as official:
  **"Buddy Cloud"**, **"Buddy Sync"**, **"Buddy Hosting"**, **"Buddy Pro"**,
  **"Managed Buddy"**.
- Use the Buddy sticker icon, or confusingly similar marks, as your own branding —
  including in app stores, domains, and social handles.
- Imply your product or service is made, endorsed, or operated by Buddy's
  maintainer when it isn't.

## Why

One codebase serves two things: the open-source project anyone can run, and the
hosted service the maintainer operates. The code being free only works long-term if
the *name* reliably points at the genuine article. Protecting the mark is what lets
the code stay MIT.

Questions or permission requests: open an issue or email the maintainer.
